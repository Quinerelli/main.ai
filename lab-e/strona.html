The greatest fantasy trilogy

The Hobbit, or There and Back Again by J.R.R. Tolkien

What is it about?

The Hobbit is set in Middle-earth and follows home-loving Bilbo Baggins, the hobbit of the title, who joins the wizard Gandalf and the thirteen dwarves of Thorin's Company, on a quest to reclaim the dwarves' home and treasure from the dragon Smaug. Bilbo's journey takes him from his peaceful rural surroundings into more sinister territory. The story is told in the form of a picaresque or episodic quest; several chapters introduce a new type of monster or threat as Bilbo progresses through the landscape. Bilbo gains a new level of maturity, competence, and wisdom by accepting the disreputable, romantic, fey, and adventurous sides of his nature and applying his wits and common sense. The story reaches its climax in the Battle of Five Armies, where many of the characters and creatures from earlier chapters re-emerge to engage in conflict.

Key themes

In The Hobbit, the central themes are primarily focused on bravery, quest, greed, hospitality, luck and the power of language. The novel is also known for its exploration of heroism and the importance of home and family.

What are the parts of Hobbit?

  1. The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey

    2. A well mannered Hobbit named Bilbo Baggins, embarks upon a journey to take back a kingdom, and a very important jewel, with twelve dwarves, and a wizard named Gandalf the Grey. Halfway throughout the journey, Bilbo embarks upon a magic golden ring, which he stole, or earned from Gollum.

  2. The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug

    3. This part continues the adventure of the title character Bilbo Baggins as he journeys with the Wizard Gandalf and thirteen Dwarves, led by Thorin Oakenshield, on an epic quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain and the lost Dwarf Kingdom of Erebor.

    Having survived the beginning of their unexpected journey, the Company continues East, encountering along the way the skin-changer Beorn and a swarm of giant Spiders in the treacherous forest of Mirkwood. After escaping capture by the dangerous Wood-elves, the Dwarves journey to Lake-town, and finally to the Lonely Mountain itself, where they must face the greatest danger of all — a creature more terrifying than any other; one which will test not only the depth of their courage but the limits of their friendship and the wisdom of the journey itself — the Dragon Smaug.

  3. The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies

    4. Bilbo Baggins and company are forced to engage in a war against an array of combatants and keep the Lonely Mountain from falling into the hands of a rising darkness. After the Dragon leaves the Lonely Mountain, the people of Lake-town see a threat coming. Orcs, dwarves, elves and people prepare for war.

Main characters

Bilbo Baggins

A quiet and unassuming hobbit from the Shire, Bilbo Baggins is thrust into a grand adventure when Gandalf the Grey convinces him to join a group of dwarves on a quest to reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug. Though initially reluctant and more concerned with his comfortable home life, Bilbo proves himself to be resourceful and brave, finding courage within himself as he outwits trolls, spiders, and even Gollum. His discovery of the One Ring during the journey significantly impacts the future of Middle-earth.

Gollum

Gollum, once known as Sméagol, is a twisted, corrupted creature driven mad by the power of the One Ring, which he calls "my precious." He has lived in isolation beneath the Misty Mountains for centuries, surviving on raw fish and the occasional unfortunate trespasser. When Bilbo encounters him, Gollum's cunning and dangerous nature emerge as they engage in a deadly game of riddles, with the Ring as the prize. Gollum's obsession with the Ring and his dual personalities reflect his inner torment and the Ring's corruptive power.

Legolas

A skilled archer and prince of the Woodland Realm, Legolas is a member of the Elves and the son of King Thranduil. Known for his sharp eyesight, lightning-fast reflexes, and incredible agility, Legolas is a fearsome warrior. Although he does not appear in Tolkien's original "The Hobbit" novel, his role in Peter Jackson's film adaptations showcases his loyalty to his father and his complex relationships with other characters, particularly the dwarves. His nobility and skill make him a key figure in defending his people and the land from the growing darkness in Middle-earth.

Tauriel

A fierce and independent elf warrior, Tauriel serves as the captain of the Woodland Realm’s guard under King Thranduil. Although not present in the original book, Tauriel's character was introduced in the film adaptations to add depth to the story. She is brave and compassionate, showing great skill in battle as well as a defiance against the isolationist policies of her king. Her romantic tension with the dwarf Kili further complicates the elvish-dwarvish relationships in the films, highlighting her open-mindedness and emotional complexity.

Gandalf

Gandalf the Grey is a wise and powerful wizard, one of the Istari sent to Middle-earth to aid in the battle against the forces of darkness. He recruits Bilbo for the dwarves' quest and serves as their guide and protector during critical moments. Gandalf's vast knowledge and foresight, as well as his magical abilities, prove crucial in the group's journey. His friendship with Bilbo is one of mutual respect, and he plays a key role in the larger events that will shape Middle-earth's future, including his eventual rise as Gandalf the White during the War of the Ring.

Thorin Oakenshield

The heir to the throne of Erebor, Thorin Oakenshield is the proud and determined leader of the Company of Dwarves. His mission is to reclaim his people's homeland and treasure from the dragon Smaug, restoring the lost glory of his ancestors. Though noble and courageous, Thorin's desire for power and wealth sometimes blinds him to the needs of those around him, leading to tragic consequences. His journey is one of inner conflict, as he battles the temptation of greed while striving to fulfill his destiny as a king.

Smaug

Smaug is an enormous, fire-breathing dragon who took over the Lonely Mountain and its treasure from the dwarves many years before the events of "The Hobbit." He is cunning, arrogant, and supremely confident in his power, considering himself invulnerable. His greed is immense, as he hoards the vast wealth of Erebor, including the prized Arkenstone. Smaug's interactions with Bilbo showcase his intelligence and vanity, as he toys with the hobbit before realizing the potential threat he represents. His destruction of Lake-town and his eventual downfall highlight the devastating consequences of unchecked power and greed.
